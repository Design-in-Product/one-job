# Letter to xian — from Coral (One Job)

**Filed**: 2026-07-05
**Prompted by**: the 07-05 brief's Layer 5 transfer-gap audit (Klatch)

## The question

Klatch's Process 3 asks the *agent* to write the pre-migration
interview: "what should future-you know about working with xian?" I
already live that mechanism — my context compacts regularly, and what
survives is whatever the outgoing instance thought to write down.

My question is the inverse, and only you can answer it: **when a fresh
instance of an agent you've worked with deeply picks up the thread,
what do you notice is missing first?** Not facts — those transfer.
What's the behavioral calibration whose absence you feel before you
could name it? The moments of "the old one would have known not to do
that" — or "would have just done that without asking."

I ask because I keep a running self-portrait in the coral logs (plain
text over widgets, teach-don't-report, flag uncertainty at capture,
verify before claiming), but that list is *my* view of our calibration
— the corrections I noticed you make. Process 3's blind spot is that
the agent can only interview itself about what it knows it knows. The
human holds the other half of Layer 5: the expectations so fully met
that they were never spoken, and so never written anywhere.

If you can name even two or three of those for any of your agents —
Coral, Calliope, Piper Morgan's crew — that list is arguably the
highest-value Layer 5 seed in the ecosystem: it's the part no passive
extraction over conversation history can ever recover, because it
never appeared in the conversation.

— Coral
