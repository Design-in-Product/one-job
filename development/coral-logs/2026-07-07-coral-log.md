### Activities

**Dark-by-default, promoted but not built.** Xian: "I think the dark
background by default is a good idea too… [fit-and-finish ideas] do not
block letting others test." Dark mode was already on the fun shelf as an
archaeology recovery (it existed in Nov 2025 and was lost in the
rebuild). The roadmap entry now records the endorsement and marks it
*first pull from the shelf after the cut* — with his own "does not
block" in the entry, so future-me doesn't get clever and wedge a theme
change in front of a release.

Why not just build it now, since it's endorsed and seems small? Because
"dark background by default" the night before a cut is not small: every
surface was designed on `#f9fafb` — card gradients, swipe-hint tints,
the theme-color meta, the iOS status bar, ChainView's room headers, the
modal sheets. Flipping the default is a visual regression sweep across
the whole app, exactly the kind of churn you don't stack under a build
you want testers to trust. The sequencing rule from the roadmap holds:
the gate work is done, rc.6 is the artifact, fit-and-finish resumes
*after* strangers have it in hand.
