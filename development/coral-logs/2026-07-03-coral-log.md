1. **Subtasks, not substacks** — any task can hold subtasks, recursively,
   ad infinitum. Named stacks-inside-tasks are a rarer, next-level
   construct we may not need at all yet. (Current code has this exactly
   inverted: nesting is only possible through the heavyweight `Substack`
   container, and only one level deep.)
2. **Done/Archive/Trash are stacks in a lifecycle chain** — from Done,
   left-swipe = un-done, right-swipe = archive; from Archive, back to
   Done or onward to Trash. Right always advances a card along the
   chain, left always moves it back. Audit gaps #2 (no delete) and #3
   (dead-end Completed) dissolve into this shape.
3. **Stacks sit on a canvas** — zoom out, swipe between neighboring
   stacks, zoom in; multiple top-level decks (health, work) live on the
   same canvas by the same mechanism. The 2025 "spatial thinking" seed,
   returned. The long-press menu was always a stand-in for views having

4. **Text entry fights the card interface** (ergonomic tension) — may

## Item 8: the reconciliation — a card is a container

Xian located where substack and subtask got confused, and the fix
unifies the whole vision: creating a subtask creates **a card within a
card** — the flipped card's face is itself a canvas holding a smaller
deck (starting with the one task you just added), added to the exact
same way as always. Canvas → deck → card → canvas → … one recursive
structure; one-card focus preserved at every depth; the named-substack
construct dissolves entirely (nothing needed naming — the card itself
was the container).

Gesture logic came with it: top-level left-swipe defers (no higher deck
exists — the canvas above holds decks, not cards); lower decks may not
defer-to-bottom the same way (you're not sorting at that depth —
flagged for re-confirmation in the discussion phase); promoting a
subtask up a level is a *different* gesture, maybe vertical swipe up,
explicitly not designed yet.

**Cross-pollination note**: watching a designer debug their own concept
model in real time — the "confusion" wasn't a missing feature but a
wrong container boundary. The moment the container moved from "named
list inside a task" to "the card itself," three open questions
(substack semantics, canvas navigation, gesture asymmetry) collapsed
into one paradigm. Concept-model bugs fix whole clusters of feature
bugs.
