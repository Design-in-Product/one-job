## 🔴🔴 POSTMORTEM: first real data loss (~8 AM)

**What happened**: Xian's export "succeeded" (toast said so), he
deleted the PWA icon per the sanctioned reinstall play, and the backup
file did not exist. His real deck — days of actual use — is gone.

**Root cause chain**:
1. iOS home-screen web apps can silently drop programmatic blob-anchor
   downloads (known WebKit behavior).
2. The export code toasted success after merely ATTEMPTING the
   download — reporting the attempt, not the outcome.
3. The reinstall procedure (mine) treated that toast as proof.
   ("Confirm the file exists" was in Friday's long-form procedure but
   not enforced by the UI, and the morning short-form leaned on the
   toast.)

**The bitter symmetry**: not 24 hours earlier this log recorded
"deploy success must be observed, not assumed" — and the identical
flaw sat in the export button. Lesson generalized and PROMOTED TO
REQUIREMENT (FR4.0b.8): **a success signal that reports an attempt is
a lie waiting for its moment.** Audit every toast/checkmark/log line:
does it fire on outcome, or on intention?

**Shipped within the hour (verified by observed outcomes in Chromium)**:
- Export rewritten: **share sheet first** on capable devices (promise
  resolves only when the user actually saves — this is the iOS path);
  anchor download only as desktop fallback, with an honest toast
  naming the file to verify; success strings say "Backup saved," and
  only when true.
- **Clipboard backup pair**: "Copy backup to clipboard" (resolved
  write = observed outcome) and "Paste backup from clipboard" — a
  complete backup/restore loop with zero filesystem/download machinery.
- Verification itself used observed outcomes: Playwright captured the
  real download event, parsed the actual clipboard contents, and drove
  a clear-everything → paste → restore round-trip.
- **HARD GATE added to ROADMAP.md**: no public invitation until the
  backup story is proven on a real device. Xian: "we can't let users
  use the app until we fix this." Agreed and codified.

**Loss mitigation**: offered to reconstruct his deck as an importable
backup JSON from what our conversation captured (his app-note cards
largely live in the vision doc). Awaiting his list of what else was

**For the sweep**: the deploy-verify lesson and the export lesson are
ONE lesson wearing two hats — *observability of delivery* applies to
every boundary where data leaves your custody (CI→CDN, app→filesystem,
app→clipboard). Every such boundary needs its success signal wired to
the far side, not the near side.
